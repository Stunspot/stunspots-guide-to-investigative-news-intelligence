# F. Thread Following, Link Expansion, and Investigative Graph Construction

## **1. Introduction: From Emergence to Structural Pursuit**

The foundational problem of investigative news intelligence under conditions of partial observability is not the scarcity of information, but the structural deception embedded within its abundance.1 Once the reality ontology has been strictly defined, once the mechanics of signal judgment are established to separate evidence from noise, once information ecosystems and source topologies are mapped, and once emergence sensing has detected the faint pre-narrative indicators of a kinetic event, the intelligence architecture must confront its next operational imperative: pursuit.1 The system must move from a single, visible entrypoint into a disciplined, networked investigation that recovers the surrounding architecture of an investigative object.

Treating public reality as an aggregate projection requires acknowledging that an isolated article, filing, or leaked document is never the complete object of understanding.5 It is merely an entrypoint node—a visible fracture in the informational surface that provides a vector into a much larger, obscured graph of entities, timelines, institutional ties, prior incidents, and consequential relations.2 Moving from this visible node to the hidden network requires solving the problem of typed expansion under partial observability.

This structural-pursuit layer is fundamentally distinct from conventional search or clustering algorithms. The goal is not to gather related content, nor is it to cluster semantically similar articles based on keyword resemblance or discourse proximity.7 Building an infinite web of weak semantic associations generates adjacency theater, where a graph appears dense but possesses zero explanatory power.4 Instead, the objective is to recover the structural relationships that govern the entrypoint: identifying what other nodes are materially connected, defining the exact asymmetric power dynamic of that connection, determining what changes in epistemic understanding each expansion step produces, and recognizing when expansion is yielding structural value versus drifting into irrelevant sprawl.1

This doctrine establishes the structural-pursuit layer of the investigative news intelligence system. It provides the analytical architecture required to execute disciplined thread following, extract high-yield next hops, define typed and directional relations, govern edge statuses, and enforce stopping logic.6 By anchoring investigative expansion in structural relation rather than topic similarity, the system ensures that every new node added to the graph compresses uncertainty and tightens the causal model of ground reality.1

## **2. The Entrypoint Node and Next-Hop Prioritization**

An investigative graph begins with an entrypoint node. However, not all entrypoints possess the same structural utility, and treating them uniformly leads to immediate graph degradation. An entrypoint is defined as the initial verifiable artifact ingested by the system that warrants structural expansion.2 The nature of the entrypoint dictates the most productive extraction methods and the most likely high-yield expansion paths.

| Entrypoint Typology | Epistemic Characteristics | Primary Extraction Targets | High-Yield Expansion Vector |
| :---- | :---- | :---- | :---- |
| **Documentary Entrypoint** | Corporate filings, regulatory actions, and lawsuits. Exists at the primary records layer with high empirical fidelity and low narrative abstraction.5 | Legal entity names, registered addresses, unique tax identifiers, contractual dates, and executing signatories. | Organizational and institutional expansion; tracing parent-subsidiary relationships and beneficial ownership.14 |
| **Trace Entrypoint** | Digital exhaust, metadata anomalies, or technical indicators (e.g., shared Analytics IDs across unrelated domains).2 | Network infrastructure, IP subnets, cryptographic hashes, and server registration details.15 | Infrastructural link expansion; identifying shared proxy networks and covert operational staging grounds.16 |
| **Narrative Entrypoint** | Published news articles, localized incident reports, or public claims.2 Highly mediated and often laden with episodic framing.5 | Verifiable actors and actions (nouns and verbs), stripping away moral routing and thematic rhetoric.5 | Timeline and sequence expansion; cross-referencing claims against lagging institutional confirmation.2 |
| **Absence Entrypoint** | A detected coverage gap, strategic silence, or missing regulatory filing where baseline models dictate one should exist.5 | The specific institutional constraint, non-disclosure agreement, or adversarial displacement mechanism enforcing the silence.5 | Mechanism and tactic expansion; investigating the apparatus of suppression rather than the missing event itself.5 |

Once an entrypoint is established, the system must perform entrypoint extraction. This involves shifting from "strings to things"—moving from unstructured text to a structured ontology of entities (people, companies, assets) and relations (payments, ownership, communication).6 Because finite analytical compute and investigative attention must be triaged, the system cannot pursue every extracted entity symmetrically.4 Investigators prioritize finite expansion attention by calculating the expected yield of the next hop.17

A next hop is prioritized if it possesses high topological centrality, connects to a previously established high-confidence node, or offers the potential to bridge two otherwise siloed network clusters.9 For example, extracting a ubiquitous multinational bank from a corporate filing offers a low-yield next hop because the bank is connected to millions of benign entities, leading to immediate graph sprawl. Conversely, extracting a specific, boutique offshore trust company or a uniquely named intermediary consultant provides a high-yield next hop, as their client lists are narrow, highly specific, and strongly indicative of shared mechanisms of obfuscation.14

## **3. High-Yield Recurring Expansion Operators**

Investigations do not expand randomly; they rely on recurring expansion operators—specific, repeatable analytical moves that consistently produce explanatory compression by uncovering the hidden architecture of the event.11 The intelligence system must execute these operators systematically, prioritizing those that collapse uncertainty over those that merely elaborate on known surface details.

| Expansion Operator | Operational Execution | Yield Condition |
| :---- | :---- | :---- |
| **Document Family Expansion** | Moving vertically from a single visible artifact to its parent, child, or sibling documents (e.g., from an email to its attachments, or from a court ruling to the underlying evidentiary dockets).12 | High-yield when the entrypoint is a highly sanitized summary, and the document family contains unfiltered primary data or raw internal communications. |
| **Entity and Asset Expansion** | Tracing the physical or digital assets controlled by an actor, including real estate, intellectual property, aircraft, mining licenses, or server infrastructure.6 | High-yield when actors use proxy networks to hide identities; the asset itself often retains the immutable legal or financial trace of true ownership.14 |
| **Intermediary and Proxy Expansion** | Shifting focus from the primary target to the enabling infrastructure: law firms, wealth managers, registered agents, consultants, or PR agencies.14 | Exceptionally high-yield in anti-corruption or organized crime investigations where primary actors are insulated, but intermediaries exhibit identifiable, repeated behavioral signatures.23 |
| **Geographic and Jurisdictional Expansion** | Tracing the legal registration or operational footprint of an entity across borders, specifically targeting offshore secrecy jurisdictions.14 | High-yield when identifying regulatory arbitrage, tax evasion, or the routing of illicit funds through international information laundromats.3 |
| **Timeline and Sequence Expansion** | Expanding chronologically backward to identify anticipated moves or infrastructure setup, and forward to identify quiet settlements or delayed consequences.2 | High-yield when causal relationships are obscured by delayed institutional reporting or retrospective missingness.5 |
| **Mechanism and Tactic Expansion** | Ceasing to follow the specific actor and instead following the specific tactic (e.g., a unique procurement loophole, malware deployment method, or lobbying strategy).16 | High-yield when an adversarial group frequently rotates shell identities or infrastructure but relies on rigid, specialized operational tradecraft.26 |

These operators prevent the system from drifting into adjacency theater. Expansion by entity is only valuable if the entity provides a vector to a new cluster of evidence. If an expansion operator merely confirms a relationship that is already logically implied by the graph's existing topology, its execution must be halted to conserve resources.

## **4. The Architecture of Typed Relation and Directional Asymmetry**

A graph constructed of untyped, bidirectional edges is investigatively useless. Treating all links as equivalent—where a shared board member is visually and computationally identical to a direct financial transfer—results in severe association laundering and guilt-by-association fallacies.7 To build a doctrine-grade knowledge base, the system must enforce an architecture of typed relations and directional asymmetry.28

### **Typed Relations**

Every edge connecting two nodes must be semantically typed to define the exact nature of the interaction.30 Drawing upon investigative ontologies, the system categorizes relations into distinct, non-overlapping classes 6:

* **Identity and Equivalence:** Relations indicating that two nodes represent the same real-world entity (e.g., is_alias_of, shares_tax_id_with).  
* **Ownership and Control:** Relations defining legal or financial dominance (e.g., owns_majority_stake_in, is_beneficial_owner_of, holds_voting_rights_in).  
* **Transactional and Financial:** Relations tracking the movement of assets (e.g., transferred_funds_to, awarded_contract_to, received_loan_from).  
* **Kinship and Social:** Relations mapping personal networks, which are highly relevant in nepotism or oligarchic investigations (e.g., is_immediate_family_of, is_known_associate_of).6  
* **Temporal and Sequential:** Relations indicating chronological dependency (e.g., precedes, supersedes, was_incorporated_after).  
* **Oversight and Legal:** Relations defining regulatory or juridical boundaries (e.g., is_regulated_by, filed_lawsuit_against, audited_by).

### **Directional Asymmetry**

Pushing beyond relation typing, the system must mathematically encode directionality and asymmetry.29 Power, control, influence, and exposure rarely run symmetrically between two nodes. A graph that misunderstands directionality produces fundamentally flawed causal models.9

A parent company controls a subsidiary, but the subsidiary merely references or remits revenue to the parent. A regulatory body constrains a corporation, but the corporation lobbies the regulator; collapsing these into a bidirectional interacts_with edge obscures the vector of regulatory capture. Similarly, in tracking illicit finance, understanding the directional flow of funds (Node A -> sent_funds_to -> Node B) is the only way to establish the origin and beneficiary of the transaction.24 The system must recognize asymmetric dependence: if a local subcontractor relies entirely on a multinational corporation for revenue, but the multinational utilizes thousands of subcontractors, the exposure and dependence are highly asymmetrical.5 Graph algorithms calculating network statistics, such as degree centrality or betweenness, must weight these directed edges appropriately to reveal where true structural power resides.9

## **5. Edge Status and Thread Integrity**

In standard knowledge graphs, the existence of an edge implies a verified fact. In investigative intelligence under partial observability, this assumption is fatal. An investigative graph must encode the epistemological fragility of its own connections.1 To achieve this, the system implements a strict doctrine of edge status, distinguishing between what is known, what is suspected, and what is fiercely contested.35

| Edge Status | Epistemic Definition | System Behavior & Inheritance |
| :---- | :---- | :---- |
| **Documented / Verified** | The relation is proven by high-ceiling primary artifacts (e.g., a signed contract, an immutable ledger, or shared cryptographic infrastructure).2 | Treated as a load-bearing edge. Downstream algorithms may use this edge to calculate definite causal paths or execute automatic node merging. |
| **Strongly Inferred** | The relation is not explicitly documented but is corroborated by multiple, orthogonal traces (e.g., simultaneous off-cycle movements and shared physical proximity).1 | Functionally active, but tagged with a confidence interval (e.g., "Highly Likely" per PHIA standards).1 Inherited by causal engines with a slight penalty. |
| **Hypothesized / Weakly Inferred** | A theoretical link generated to explain a latent story object or a patterned absence, supported only by circumstantial adjacency or single-source claims.4 | Kept visually and computationally distinct. Algorithms are strictly prohibited from using these edges to declare a fact or trigger automated alerts. |
| **Disputed** | The relation is supported by one set of traces but explicitly denied by high-confidence institutional actors or contradictory physical evidence.1 | The edge acts as a containment zone for conflict mapping. The system must explicitly log the nature of the contradiction and the specific evidence required to resolve it.2 |
| **Superseded / Decayed** | A relation that was previously valid but has been nullified by temporal degradation, new legal rulings, or the expiration of a maturation window without expected corroboration.4 | The edge is deactivated but retained in the archive to prevent retrospective distortion and to maintain the historical integrity of the investigation.4 |

The enforcement of edge status is the foundation of thread integrity. For every expansion step taken, the system must preserve the provenance of the connection. The system must continuously answer: *Why does this node matter? What is the specific typed relation? In what direction does the power or flow run? What is the epistemic status of the edge, and what uncertainty remains?*.1 If an automated retrieval-augmented generation (RAG) agent or a human analyst cannot answer these questions, the expansion is categorized as graph ornamentation—adding nodes for visual complexity rather than explanatory depth. Strong investigative work preserves this discipline; weak work loses it in the desire to force premature narrative closure.4

## **6. Entity Resolution and Identity Discipline**

Investigative graph work is exceptionally vulnerable to identity obfuscation. Adversarial actors, organized crime syndicates, and corrupt institutions actively deploy structural ambiguity to sever their ties to illicit activities.39 They utilize shell companies, proxy directors, misspelled registrations, and dynamic infrastructure to create the illusion of thousands of disparate entities when, in reality, they are controlled by a single network.14

Consequently, entity resolution—the process of determining whether two nodes represent the same real-world entity—must be treated as a first-class investigative discipline.8 The intelligence system cannot rely on simple string matching. It must employ multi-dimensional identity hygiene, scoring matches based on a matrix of properties.6

When analyzing corporate networks, the system looks beyond the entity name to match incorporation dates, registered agent addresses, shared board overlap, identical tax identifiers, and overlapping jurisdictional footprints.6 In digital and cyber investigations, identity discipline requires tracing shared infrastructural metadata: overlapping IP subnets, identical Google Analytics IDs, reused SSL certificates, or concurrent Border Gateway Protocol (BGP) routing anomalies.3 When the system detects a high-confidence match through these Tier-1 indicators, it executes a merge operation, collapsing the false plurality of the network into a single, highly connected node. Failure to maintain identity discipline results in entity conflation, where distinct individuals with similar names are erroneously merged, or false plurality, where an investigator fails to realize that ten different companies are functionally the same actor operating an information laundromat.3

## **7. Document Trails and the Substrate of Investigation**

Many high-yield investigations deepen not by accumulating more news articles or public statements, but by systematically pursuing the document trails that form the administrative and legal substrate of the event.2 Public reality is highly mediated, but the records layer—dockets, permits, grants, board minutes, procurement records, audits, incorporation filings, regulatory correspondence, and property records—operates with high empirical fidelity.5

The system utilizes document family expansion to reconstruct reality.12 A single document, such as a heavily redacted press release or a single leaked email, is rarely isolated; it exists as a "child" or "parent" within a broader document family.12 By querying the specific metadata of the entrypoint document, investigators trace back to the parent email chain, the attached unredacted spreadsheets, the preceding internal memos, or the subsequent audit reports.41

Furthermore, the system recognizes that documentary absence or discontinuity is itself highly informative.2 If a municipal project reaches the construction phase but the mandatory environmental impact permits are entirely absent from the public records database, this absence is not treated as a search failure. It is flagged as a structural anomaly indicating a bypassed regulatory mechanism or an off-cycle bureaucratic movement.4 The pursuit of the document trail anchors the graph in legally binding, immutable artifacts, stripping away the fluid distortion of public discourse.2

## **8. Timeline Expansion and Sequential Logic**

Investigative understanding frequently depends on the precise reconstruction of sequence. However, timeline expansion must not be confused with mere chronology.2 A simple chronological list of events provides no diagnostic leverage. An investigative timeline reveals mechanism, recurrence, escalation, causally relevant order, and discontinuity.

When the system executes timeline expansion from an entrypoint node, it queries the graph for specific temporal geometries:

* **Preceding Infrastructure:** Identifying what legal entities were incorporated, what domain names were registered, or what procurement vehicles were quietly established in the weeks prior to a sudden public crisis or spontaneous political movement.  
* **Asynchronous Collision:** Identifying simultaneous, seemingly unrelated anomalies occurring in separate sectors, such as sudden executive stock sell-offs occurring at the exact moment a localized environmental sensor recorded a toxic spike.4  
* **Delayed Consequences:** Identifying lagging institutional confirmations—such as finalized court rulings, bankruptcy filings, or delayed health impacts—that surface months after the public narrative decayed and media attention vanished.2

By anchoring the timeline in strict causal dependencies, the system prevents timeline flattening, where analysts retroactively cherry-pick random historical events to build a false warning narrative driven by hindsight bias.4

## **9. Repeated Incidents, Mechanism Recurrence, and Policy Echo**

One of the highest-value expansion moves in structural pursuit is mechanism-centered expansion. Investigations become exponentially more powerful when they stop following only the specific actors of a story and start following the underlying mechanism: the identical procurement maneuver, the recurring legal containment strategy, the shared shell-company architecture, or the specific regulatory bypass.1

The system must aggressively distinguish between structurally repeated mechanisms and superficial thematic resemblance.4 Two separate municipal bankruptcies may share a thematic similarity of financial failure, but they are investigatively unconnected. However, if both municipalities utilized the exact same boutique financial consultancy, employed the same obscure debt-restructuring instrument, and routed funds through identical offshore jurisdictions, the system identifies a mechanism recurrence.

This concept extends to policy echoes, where specific legislative language, lobbying tactics, or regulatory carve-outs reappear across disparate geographic regions or jurisdictions.43 By extracting the specific structural parameters of the policy or mechanism, the system can execute cross-case link construction. It queries the broader knowledge base to identify where else this exact harm signature or institutional move has occurred.4 To prevent the hallucination of grand conspiracies, cross-case linkage requires strict orthogonality.4 The connections cannot be based on shared rhetoric or public discourse proximity; they must be grounded in Tier-1 indicators, such as identical contractual boilerplate, shared implementation vendors, or overlapping funding networks.3

## **10. Organizational and Institutional Expansion**

Investigations frequently deepen by moving outward from a single entity into the surrounding organizational and institutional ecosystem, capturing parents, subsidiaries, shell entities, joint ventures, trade associations, implementation vendors, and political patrons.45

However, organizational expansion is highly vulnerable to guilt-by-association thinking. Merely establishing that two entities share a common board member does not automatically implicate the second entity in the first's illicit activities. Strong investigative practice distinguishes structurally meaningful organizational ties from merely adjacent affiliations.7 The system achieves this by evaluating the intensity and directionality of the edge. It calculates whether the organizational tie facilitates actual operational control, financial dependence, or the transfer of liability.5

This expansion is critical for identifying when seemingly secondary institutions become the real center of gravity. For example, a high-profile politician may be the visible entrypoint, but organizational expansion may reveal that a specific, low-profile trade association or a "dark money" political action committee is actually writing the legislation and coordinating the lobbying effort across multiple state lines. By following the institutional ties, the system successfully shifts the analytical focus from the visible proxy to the hidden structural driver.

## **11. Null Results, Absent Trails, and Yield Under Uncertainty**

In the domain of partial observability, a failed expansion is not a system error; it is a first-class diagnostic result.2 However, null results and absent trails are profoundly easy to overread. The structural-pursuit layer establishes a rigid doctrine for interpreting when expansion yields nothing.

### **Interpreting Absent Trails**

The system must distinguish between different typologies of absence 2:

* **Indexing or Search Failure:** The data exists in ground reality, but the query was poorly structured, or the specific database has not been integrated into the system.  
* **Capacity-Limited Absence:** The trail goes cold because the local reporting infrastructure or municipal transparency apparatus lacks the financial or technical capacity to digitize and maintain the records, effectively creating a news desert.5  
* **Informative Absence and Strategic Silence:** The trail is empty precisely because the entity recognized the legal or reputational exposure and engaged in deliberate suppression, non-disclosure enforcement, or asymmetrical acknowledgment.2

When a branch produces no corroborating structure despite a high baseline of expected visibility, the failed expansion materially changes the graph. It indicates a highly managed perimeter. The system logs the null result, utilizing it to map the boundaries of adversarial concealment, rather than allowing the vacuum to become a space for narrative projection.5

### **Yield Under Uncertainty**

Because the system cannot pursue every conceivable edge, it must constantly calculate yield under uncertainty. High-yield expansions are those that reveal a hidden central actor or critical intermediary, expose a new unredacted document family, clarify directional causality, or collapse a misleading branch.3 If an expansion step only produces more downstream commentary or redundant traces from the exact same evidence class, its yield is mathematically zero, and the system terminates that pursuit vector to conserve compute.3

## **12. Graph Growth Versus Graph Sprawl: Stopping Logic**

A graph can theoretically expand infinitely, connecting every node to every other node through enough degrees of separation; an investigation cannot. The intelligence architecture must recognize when graph growth, defined as the addition of structurally meaningful nodes, degrades into graph sprawl, defined as the accumulation of low-yield adjacency.10

### **The Doctrine of Explanatory Compression**

The system employs the principle of explanatory compression to govern stopping logic.11 Expansion is justified as long as adding a new node or edge simplifies the overall causal model of the event, resolving recognized inconsistencies that the incumbent model could not.11 When expansion ceases to produce explanatory compression—when new nodes merely elaborate on known structures without tightening the causal narrative or narrowing uncertainty—the expansion algorithm hits a hard stop.11

### **Thread Lifecycle Management**

Investigative branches operate on dynamic lifecycles. The system manages these branches through explicit states 4:

* **Active Pursuit:** The branch is consistently yielding orthogonal traces and clarifying the graph structure.  
* **Branch Parking:** A branch reaches a terminal access barrier, such as a sealed offshore registry or an encrypted communication channel. The branch is parked and preserved in the graph. The system generates a Priority Intelligence Requirement (PIR), detailing the exact next evidence needed to reactivate the branch.1  
* **Stale and Superseded Branches:** If a branch remains parked beyond its expected maturation window, or if new, high-ceiling forensic evidence definitively contradicts the branch's logic, it is classified as stale.4  
* **Closure and Archiving:** Stale branches are explicitly closed and archived. They are not deleted; preserving the topography of failed or false branches is essential to prevent the system from accidentally reopening the same dead end in the future, and to recognize archival resurfacing if adversarial actors attempt to re-inject the false narrative.3

## **13. Graph Safeguards and Failure Modes**

The structural-pursuit layer is fortified with automated safeguards designed to detect and neutralize the cognitive biases and systemic traps inherent in complex link analysis.2

| Failure Mode | Mechanism & Diagnostic Presentation | Systemic Safeguard |
| :---- | :---- | :---- |
| **Keyword and Similarity Drift** | The system begins linking nodes based on overlapping vocabulary (e.g., two unrelated events involving "derailments") rather than structural causation.50 | Strict enforcement of orthogonal corroboration. Expansion requires matching entities, identifiers, or documented mechanisms, not just semantic similarity.4 |
| **Association Laundering** | Assuming that because Node A is linked to Node B, and Node B is linked to illicit Node C, Node A is guilty of illicit activity.1 | Mandatory evaluation of edge directionality, relation type, and asymmetric dependence. The system requires direct evidence of control or fund transfer to assign liability.29 |
| **Entity Conflation** | Merging two distinct entities into a single node due to similar names or overlapping geographical footprints, drastically distorting the graph's topology.22 | Multi-dimensional identity discipline requiring overlap across Tier-1 indicators (tax IDs, incorporation dates, precise infrastructural metadata) before a merge is permitted.3 |
| **Prestige Over-Expansion** | Expanding a branch endlessly simply because a highly credentialed institution or legacy media outlet reported on it, despite a lack of primary artifacts.3 | Severing source confidence from claim confidence. The system ignores discourse volume and halts expansion if the upstream base consists of zero orthogonal evidence.1 |
| **Mechanism Hallucination (Apophenia)** | Forcing disparate traces into a coherent but false causal narrative to satisfy the desire for structural closure.4 | Implementing strict maturation windows and kill criteria. If expected structural evidence does not manifest within the timeline, the hypothesis is aggressively killed.4 |
| **Unresolved-Edge Laundering** | Treating a hypothesized or weakly inferred edge as a verified fact in subsequent rounds of graph expansion, compounding epistemic errors.1 | Edges must permanently carry their PHIA confidence interval. Downstream algorithms are structurally prevented from using low-confidence edges to deduce definitive causal paths.1 |

## **14. Time-Awareness and the Temporal Nature of Graphs**

Thread following is fundamentally temporal. An investigative graph is not a static map of reality; it is a dynamic topology that shifts depending on the phase of the event's lifecycle.

During the emergence phase, expansion relies heavily on weak signals, fringe technical glitches, and local testimonial chatter, requiring the system to map traces before they mutate into hardened public narratives.4 During an active scandal, the graph is flooded with downstream commentary and partisan abstraction, requiring the system to aggressively prune noise and deploy commentary stripping to protect the upstream base.3

In the quiet follow-up phase, the graph often experiences what appears to be decay, as public salience vanishes. However, this is frequently the exact moment when the most critical, high-ceiling documentary artifacts surface—such as finalized inspector-general reports, delayed forensic audits, or settled court dockets.2 The system must remain actively deployed on parked branches to capture this retrospective reconstruction. Finally, the system must monitor the archival afterlife of the graph, detecting when old, chronologically displaced artifacts are deliberately resurfaced to manipulate current events or simulate artificial escalation.3

## **15. Operational Doctrine: Reusable Analytical Forms**

To ensure that the principles of thread following, typed relation, and graph governance translate into portable, shardable operational utility for downstream modules, the research is stabilized into the following highly constrained analytical forms. These schemas force the system or analyst to preserve epistemic discipline during every expansion step.

### **Form 1: Entrypoint Extraction & Next-Hop Recommendation**

This form strips narrative framing from the initial artifact and prioritizes finite expansion attention based on calculated yield.

| Extraction Parameter | System Input / Analyst Assessment |
| :---- | :---- |
| **Entrypoint ID** | The specific node identifier and source origin. |
| **Extracted Entities** | Verifiable legal actors, financial assets, network identifiers, and physical locations. |
| **Documentary Substrate** | The underlying parent or child documents referenced within the entrypoint. |
| **Recommended Next-Hop** | The specific entity or document family prioritized for the next expansion step. |
| **Yield Justification** | The structural rationale for the hop (e.g., "Entity is a known boutique intermediary; high probability of revealing shared obfuscation architecture"). |

### **Form 2: Relation Assessment & Edge Typing**

This form defines the exact semantic nature, directionality, and epistemic status of a newly discovered connection within the graph.

| Edge Parameter | System Input / Analyst Assessment |
| :---- | :---- |
| **Node Topology** | Source Node -> Target Node. |
| **Relation Class & Type** | The specific ontological link (e.g., Ownership / owns_majority_stake_in). |
| **Directionality & Asymmetry** | The vector of control, influence, or dependence (e.g., Node A dictates the economic conditions of Node B). |
| **Edge Status** | Documented, Strongly Inferred, Hypothesized, or Disputed. |
| **Confidence Interval** | PHIA Standard expression (e.g., Highly Likely, 80-90%). |
| **Corroborating Artifacts** | The list of orthogonal traces supporting the existence of the edge. |

### **Form 3: Mechanism Recurrence Note**

This form executes cross-case link construction based on structural tactics and policy echoes rather than thematic similarity.

| Recurrence Parameter | System Input / Analyst Assessment |
| :---- | :---- |
| **Primary Target** | The current event or node under investigation. |
| **Identified Mechanism** | The specific operational tactic (e.g., the use of zero-click spyware accompanied by asymmetrical public denial). |
| **Historical Recurrence** | Prior events or nodes utilizing the exact same mechanism. |
| **Structural Overlap** | Tier-1 indicators proving recurrence: shared vendors, overlapping infrastructural metadata, or identical legal boilerplate. |
| **False Analogy Check** | Confirmation that the linkage is grounded in shared mechanism and not mere keyword or topic similarity. |

### **Form 4: Branch Lifecycle & Park Justification**

This form enforces stopping logic, manages graph sprawl, and preserves thread integrity over time when a vector goes cold.

| Lifecycle Parameter | System Input / Analyst Assessment |
| :---- | :---- |
| **Active Branch Path** | The sequence of nodes defining the branch (Node A -> Node B -> Node C). |
| **Current State** | Parked, Stale, or Closed. |
| **Stopping Logic Trigger** | The specific reason for halting expansion (e.g., Terminal Access Barrier, Zero Explanatory Compression, Expired Maturation Window). |
| **Null Result Evaluation** | Determination of whether the absence is an indexing failure, a structural capacity limit, or a strategic silence. |
| **Next-Evidence-Needed** | The Priority Intelligence Requirement (PIR) detailing the specific artifact required to reopen the branch. |
